Dr. Normand Borlaug

The man who saved a billion lives

Dr. Normand Borlaug,third from the left, trains biologist in mexico on how to increase what yields - part of his life-long war on hunger.

Here's a time line of Dr. Borlaungs life:

  • 1914 - Born in Cresco, Iowa
  • 1933 - Leavesz his family's farm to attend the univerty of Minessota, thanks to Depression era program known as the "National Youth Administration."
  • 1935 - Has to stop school and save up more money. Works in the Civilian Conservation Corps. helping starving Americans "I saw how food changed them" He said "All of this left scars on me.
  • 1937 - Finishes University and take a job at the US forestry service
  • 1938 - Marries wife of 61 years Margret Gibson. Get laid off due to budget cuts. Inspired by Elvin Chalse Stalkman, He return to school study under stakman. Who teaches him about breeding pest resistent plant.
  • 1941 - Tries to enroll in the ministry after pearl Harbor attack, but he is rejected. Instead the ministry asked his lab to walk on waterproof glue. DCC to control malaria, disinfenctant, and other applied sciences
  • 1942 - Recieves a P.hD in Genetics and plant pathology
  • 1944 - Rejects a 100% salaryincrease from Dupont, lives behind his pregnant wife and flies to mexico to head a plant pathology program. Over the next 16 years his team breeds over 6,000 different stains of disease resistent wheat. Including various verieties for major planets on earth.
  • 1945 - Discover a way to grow wheat twice each season. Doubling wheat yield.
  • 1953 _ Crosses a short, sturdy dwarf breed of wheat with a high yielding American breed, creatin a strain that respond well to fertilizer.It goes on to produce 95% of Mexico wheat
  • 1962 - Visits Delhi and brings his high yielding strin of wheat to th Indian sub-continent in time to help reduce mass starvation due to rapidly increasing population.
  • 1970 - Recieves the nobel peace price.
  • 1983 - Helps seven African countries increase thier wheat and soghum yields
  • 1984 - Becomes a distinguish professor in Texas A&M University
  • 2005 - States "We will have to double the Worlds food by 2050". Argued that genetically modified crop are ther only way we can meet the demand as we run out of arable land. States that GM crops are not inherntly dangerouus because " we've been genetically modifying plant and animal fo a long time. Long before we call it science, people were selecting the best breeds."
  • 2009 - Dies at age 95.
